- **Serotonin syndrome**: Serotonin syndrome presents with a triad of altered mental status, autonomic instability, and neuromuscular hyperactivity (especially clonus and hyperreflexia). It typically develops within 24 hours of initiating or combining serotonergic agents.
- **Verapamil**: Verapamil causes constipation.
- **Enoxaparin**: In pregnant patients diagnosed with deep vein thrombosis (DVT), low molecular weight heparin (LMWH) such as enoxaparin is the treatment of choice due to its inability to cross the placenta, predictable dosing, and favorable safety profile. Unfractionated heparin (UFH) is an alternative when rapid reversal is needed or in cases of renal impairment. Direct oral anticoagulants (DOACs) and warfarin are contraindicated due to fetal harm, and aspirin is ineffective for treating venous thrombosis.

This mechanism improves glycemic control in type 2 diabetes but carries risks such as weight gain, fluid retention, heart failure exacerbation, and hepatotoxicity.
- **GABA-B receptor agonist**: Baclofen is a GABA-B receptor agonist used to treat muscle spasticity, especially in upper motor neuron conditions like multiple sclerosis. It reduces muscle tone by inhibiting excitatory neurotransmission in the spinal cord. Baclofen is the first-line treatment for MS-related spasticity and clonus.

- **Inhibits fibrinolysis through plasminogen activation blockade**: Pioglitazone activates PPAR-γ to enhance insulin sensitivity in adipose tissue, muscle, and the liver. In contrast, inhibitors (e.g., amiodarone, cimetidine, fluconazole, metronidazole, TMP-SMX) slow warfarin metabolism, raising INR and bleeding risk. PPIs are highly effective when used appropriately, but their efficacy can be overwhelmed by large-volume meals that distend the stomach and increase reflux. Before escalating therapy or ordering invasive investigations, physicians should assess dietary habits and counsel patients on lifestyle modifications.
- **Preexisting clotting factors must first be cleared**: Warfarin inhibits vitamin K–dependent clotting factor synthesis in the liver, but its anticoagulant effect is delayed because it does not affect clotting factors already present in circulation. These preexisting factors—especially factor II (prothrombin), which has a long half-life—must degrade before INR rises. As a result, full therapeutic effect may take several days despite appropriate dosing.
- **Hyperbaric oxygen therapy**: Carbon monoxide poisoning should be suspected in winter months when patients present with headache, confusion, and nonspecific chest discomfort. Hyperbaric oxygen therapy is indicated in patients with neurologic symptoms or myocardial involvement. Pulse oximetry may appear normal despite significant tissue hypoxia, so clinical suspicion must guide management.
- **Discontinue lisinopril 24 hours prior**: Acute intermittent porphyria is caused by porphobilinogen deaminase deficiency, leading to neurotoxic buildup of ALA and PBG. Treatment with hemin downregulates ALA synthase, the rate-limiting enzyme in heme synthesis, to reduce precursor accumulation. Clinical findings such as abdominal pain, neuropathy, and dark urine without anemia or photosensitivity help distinguish it from other porphyrias or lead poisoning.

### Q1 - General
- Answer: **D. Inhibits microtubule polymerization during M phase**
- Bottom line: Vincristine inhibits microtubule polymerization, arresting cells in M phase (metaphase) and disrupting mitosis. Its mechanism explains both its anticancer effects and its classic neurologic toxicity (e.g., peripheral neuropathy). Biochemistry (General Principles) (Cell and molecular biology)
### Q2 - General
- Answer: **B. Colchicine**

### Q3 - General
- Answer: **D. Fenofibrate**
- Bottom line: Fibrates are the most effective agents at lowering TGAs. They're generally used with TGAs over 300 mg/dL. They can cause myopathy and hepatotoxicity, with increased risk when combined with statins due to a P-450 interaction. Fibrates can also cause cholesterol stones. Biochemistry (General Principles) (Lipid metabolism)
### Q4 - General
- Answer: **B. Fibric acid derivatives**
- Bottom line: Hypertriglyceridemia-induced pancreatitis develops when TG >1,000 mg/dL cause chylomicron-mediated pancreatic injury. Acute care is supportive with possible rapid TG lowering; long-term prevention requires TG <500 mg/dL, best achieved with fibrates as first-line therapy. Biochemistry (General Principles) (Lipid metabolism)
### Q5 - General
- Answer: **D. Inhibition of HMG-CoA reductase**
- Bottom line: Statin-induced myopathy occurs due to HMG-CoA reductase inhibition, which lowers cholesterol and coenzyme Q10, impairing muscle energy production. It causes proximal muscle pain with elevated CK soon after therapy begins. Prompt detection and stopping the drug prevent rhabdomyolysis and kidney injury. Biochemistry (General Principles) (Lipid metabolism)
### Q6 - General
- Answer: **A. Recombinant α-galactosidase**
- Bottom line: Fabry disease is an X-linked recessive lysosomal storage disorder caused by α-galactosidase A deficiency, leading to ceramide trihexoside accumulation in vascular endothelium, kidneys, heart, and peripheral nerves. Clinical features include angiokeratomas, acroparesthesias, hypohidrosis, proteinuric renal disease, and left ventricular hypertrophy. Recombinant α-galactosidase is the specific treatment and should be initiated early to reduce progression and preserve organ function. Biochemistry (General Principles) (Lysosomal storage diseases)
### Q7 - General
- Answer: **B. ↑ Adenylate cyclase activity and ↑ cyclic AMP**
- Bottom line: β-blocker overdose causes bradycardia and hypotension by blocking β-adrenergic receptors and reducing intracellular cAMP. Glucagon bypasses this blockade by directly activating adenylate cyclase, increasing cAMP, and restoring heart rate and contractility independent of β-receptor signaling. This makes it the antidote of choice in hemodynamically unstable β-blocker toxicity. Biochemistry (General Principles) (Receptors & extracellular matrix molecules)

### Q12 - General

It typically presents 1-8 weeks after ART initiation in patients with low baseline CD4 count. Treatment includes continuation of ART, anti-infective therapy, and sometimes steroids for inflammation control. Infectious Diseases (HIV and sexually transmitted infections)

### Q17 - General
- Answer: **A. Amiodarone**

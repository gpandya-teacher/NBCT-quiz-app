## Fracture/Trauma
- Basilar skull fractures often present with Battle sign, raccoon eyes, and CSF rhinorrhea or otorrhea. Associated Cushing reflex (hypertension, bradycardia, irregular respirations) suggests increased ICP. Diagnosis is largely clinical, but CT is confirmatory.

## Muscle/Histology
- The A-band represents the full length of thick myosin filaments and does not change in length during contraction. The I-band (actin only), H-zone (myosin only), and sarcomere length all shorten during contraction.
- Inclusion body myositis is a slowly progressive inflammatory myopathy seen in patients over 50, often with both proximal and distal weakness. ## Spine/Back
- Cervical foraminal stenosis is caused by nerve root compression at the neural foramina due to degenerative changes. Presents with unilateral weakness, sensory loss, and pain in a dermatomal/radicular distribution without signs of cord compression. MRI of the cervical spine is the diagnostic test of choice to visualize foraminal narrowing and nerve root impingement.

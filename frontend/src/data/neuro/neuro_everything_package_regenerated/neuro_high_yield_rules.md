# Neurology High-Yield Rules

- Crossed face/body findings usually localize to the brainstem.
- Leg-predominant weakness points medial cortex; face/arm-predominant weakness points lateral cortex.
- Patellar reflex localizes to L4; Achilles reflex localizes to S1.
- Wrist drop after midshaft humerus fracture means radial nerve until proven otherwise.
- Inability to abduct the eye means CN VI; down-and-out eye with ptosis and mydriasis means CN III.
- Trigeminal neuralgia causes brief electric shock pain triggered by touch or chewing.
- Cluster headache causes severe unilateral periorbital pain with autonomic symptoms and restlessness.
- Bell palsy is a lower motor neuron facial palsy involving the forehead.
- Brown-Séquard = ipsilateral motor/vibration loss + contralateral pain/temperature loss.
- Anterior cord syndrome = bilateral motor and pain/temperature loss with preserved vibration/proprioception.

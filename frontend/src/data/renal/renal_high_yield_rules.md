# Renal High-Yield Rules

## AKI patterns

- Prerenal azotemia: **high BUN/Cr, low FeNa, concentrated urine**.
- ATN: **low/normal BUN/Cr relative to prerenal, high FeNa, muddy brown casts**.
- Rhabdomyolysis clue: **heme-positive dipstick with few/no RBCs + high CK + hyperkalemia**.
- Proximal tubule is one of the first nephron sites injured by ischemia.

## Glomerular disease anchors

- Minimal change disease: child with nephrotic syndrome, **podocyte foot process effacement**, steroid responsive.
- PSGN: nephritic syndrome after strep, **low C3** and subepithelial humps.
- MPGN: nephritic/nephrotic mix with **tram-track** GBM.
- Membranous nephropathy: nephrotic syndrome with **spike-and-dome** and subepithelial IgG/C3.

## Urinary obstruction + anatomy

- UPJ obstruction: unilateral hydronephrosis with normal VCUG.
- Most common stone lodgment site: **ureterovesical junction**.
- Horseshoe kidney: **fusion of inferior poles**, trapped under IMA.
- Congenital VUR predisposes to **cortical scarring/reflux nephropathy**.

## Electrolyte + acid-base rules

- Vomiting / pyloric stenosis / hyperemesis: **hypochloremic, hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis** with compensatory high CO2.
- Diarrhea: **normal anion gap metabolic acidosis** with low bicarbonate and often low potassium.
- DKA: **high anion gap metabolic acidosis** with low bicarbonate.
- Salicylates after ~20 min: **mixed respiratory alkalosis + anion gap metabolic acidosis**.
- CKD/uremia: **high anion gap metabolic acidosis + hyperkalemia + hyperphosphatemia**.
- Primary hyperaldosteronism / secondary hyperaldosteronism: **hypokalemic metabolic alkalosis**.

## Urinary incontinence patterns

- Overflow incontinence in diabetes: **impaired detrusor contractility from autonomic neuropathy**.
- Urge incontinence in MS: **uninhibited detrusor contractions**.
- Interstitial cystitis: chronic urgency/frequency with sterile cultures, pain worse with filling and better after voiding.
